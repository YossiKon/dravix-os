Feature map

Everything it can do.

Two halves of one creature: what runs on the robot (the dravix ESPHome firmware) and what runs in the add-on (the service + dashboard).

On the robot — the firmware

🐣

Drawn pet face

Two big eyes that blink, look around, squint when happy and widen when listening — plus cheeks and an animated mouth. Every expression has its own LED colour and head pose.

🎭

7 on-device modes

awake · morning · focus · quiet · night · busy · sleep — each reshapes the face, LEDs, volume and autonomous behaviour.

🗣️

Voice + speech bubble

On-device wake word “Okay, Nabu” hands off to HA Assist (STT/LLM/TTS); replies pop into a comic bubble with a tail to the mouth.

🎩

Themes & accessories

Dress-up: 7 face backdrops and 22 accessories — glasses, top hat, crown, cat ears, boxing gloves… opt-in, never auto-applied.

🌬️

Breathing & body language

The face rises and settles on a slow sine while idle and sways while talking, with sparse, natural idle glances.

🕹️

Games arcade

Eleven built-in games — from Simon to a tiny Doom raycaster — with a high-score board.

💗

Vitals & a real life

Energy, food, fun and calm as live bars. It drifts, and it looks after itself when a need bottoms out.

🔒

Privacy mode

Kills the mic on-device and blocks the camera, with a red PRIVACY badge you can see across the room.

🔋

Real battery gauge

Level + charging straight from the AXP2101 fuel gauge, a time-left estimate, and sleep-breathing LEDs on the charger.

📡

IR blaster + receiver

Controls your AC as a ready climate entity — and glances over, curious, when anyone uses a TV/AC remote.

🗂️

3 interactive cards

Pick any HA entities; up to four tappable rows per card toggle lights, press buttons, run scripts & scenes right on the robot.

🌐

Dashboard page

A live screenshot of any Home Assistant dashboard on its own swipe page, refreshed every 15 seconds.

In the add-on — the service & dashboard

🪞

Live face mirror

The robot's face, state and speech mirrored in the browser — bilingual (English · עברית RTL), mobile-friendly.

💬

Chat with memory

Talk to the robot by text; it remembers facts you tell it and answers to its own name.

🛡️

Security guard mode

Arm it and the robot becomes a guard camera: periodic snapshots, a slow head patrol, live teleop and a browsable gallery.

💗

Life & wellness

Needs bars with feed/rest/play/calm, plus wellness nudges for you — 20-20-20 eye breaks, stand up, hydrate.

🧠

Pluggable AI brain

HA Assist by default; switch to Claude, OpenAI or Ollama. Emotion tags in replies drive the face automatically.

🤖

MCP server

Its own MCP server lets any AI agent (Claude Desktop/Code) drive the robot, modes and your whole home.

🔌

Auto-wired entities

Discovery finds every robot entity at startup — face, servos, mode, speaker, LEDs, camera, sensors. Nothing to map.

💾

Backup & restore

Download or upload all your config — personas, routines, memories, schedule, voices — as one JSON file.

A robot with a life

It has needs, moods, and a mind of its own.

Inspired by EMO and Vector: a persistent temperament that drifts over time, reacts to being talked to and petted, and survives restarts.

Tamagotchi-style needs

Four needs, 0–100, on the robot's own VITALS screen and the dashboard. They drift down; you top them up — and petting and talking feed them too.

🍎 food
😄 fun
🧘 calm

It looks after itself — sleeps when tired, feeds itself, cheers itself up. And a hard do-not-disturb rule keeps it fully silent in focus · quiet · night · busy · sleep.

From a stock robot to alive

Set it up in six steps.

No prior ESPHome experience needed. Updates are one press afterwards.

need · M5Stack StackChan (CoreS3) need · Home Assistant on an always-on box need · the ESPHome Device Builder add-on need · a USB-C cable (first flash only)
Step 0

Back up the original firmware

A byte-for-byte snapshot of the robot as it left the factory — you can always go back. One esptool read_flash command. Do not skip.

Step 1

Create the tiny ESPHome config

In ESPHome Device Builder, make a new device and paste the ~60-line git stub. Edit the two Wi-Fi lines — it pulls the real firmware from the repo on every build.

Step 2

First flash over USB-C

Plug in → Install → Plug into this computer. The first build compiles a wake-word model, so it takes a while. Every future update is wireless.

Step 3

Adopt it in Home Assistant

HA discovers the ESPHome device automatically. Recommended: rename it to Dravix so the entity ids match the firmware's default prefix.

Step 4

Install the dravix-os add-on

Add-on Store → ⋮ → Repositories → add github.com/YossiKon/dravix-os → install → Start. It authenticates itself and auto-discovers every robot entity.

Step 5

Open the dashboard and say hi

Click Open Web UI (or http://<home-assistant>:8800). Pet the robot's head — it will like that.

Updating & rollback. Firmware update = press Install in ESPHome again (the tiny file always pulls the latest). The add-on updates itself in Home Assistant. Roll back firmware by pinning ref: v<version> from Releases, or restore your Step-0 backup to go fully factory.

No robot yet? Everything runs against a built-in mock driver — explore the whole dashboard fully offline.
